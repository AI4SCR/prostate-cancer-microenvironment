## Biological key findings

- CAFs are framed as abundant stromal drivers of prostate cancer initiation, progression, castration resistance, and metastatic spread.
- Reactive stroma in prostate cancer is characterized by loss of smooth-muscle features, increased vimentin-positive CAF populations, and extracellular-matrix remodeling.
- CAF heterogeneity is emphasized as a major biological theme, including six prostate CAF subpopulations from cited scRNA-seq studies.
- Spatial transcriptomics from cited work suggested region-specific stromal states linked to metabolism, oxidative stress, hypoxia, and inflammation.
- Multiple stromal signaling axes are highlighted, including TGF-beta, IL-6, FGF, HGF, GDF15, YAP, Hedgehog, Nox4/ROS, Eph-ephrin signaling, and exosomal miR-409.
- Bone-metastatic niches were described as involving CAF-like stromal programs and marker signatures such as POSTN, ASPN, SPARC, and PDGFRB.
- High stromal infiltration and CAF-marker programs are summarized as being associated with worse prognosis and metastatic progression.
